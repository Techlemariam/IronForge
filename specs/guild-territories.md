# 🗺️ Feature Specification: Guild Territories

**Author:** `@analyst`
**Date:** 2026-02-28
**Status:** DRAFT (Ready for `@architect`)
**Epic:** Social Engagement & Retention (Season 2)

## 📌 Executive Summary

Guild Territories introduces a global, persistent map where Guilds compete to hold specific zones. Holding a zone grants passive benefits (e.g., +5% XP, +10% Gold drop rate) to all guild members. Control is resolved weekly on Sundays based on the total Power Rating/Activity generated by the guild during that week.

---

## 📖 User Stories (INVEST)

### 1. The Territory Map

**As a** Guild Member
**I want to** view a global map of all conquerable territories and see which guild currently holds them
**So that** I know where my guild should focus its efforts.

### 2. Territory Targeting (The Warlord's Call)

**As a** Guild Leader
**I want to** select one (1) Territory as our "Active Target" for the week
**So that** all XP and Power Rating generated by my guild members contributes to conquering that specific zone.

### 3. Passive Yields (The Reward)

**As a** Guild Member
**I want to** receive a passive global buff (e.g., +10% Gold) automatically as long as my guild holds a specific territory
**So that** I feel the tangible benefits of our collective effort.

### 4. The Weekly Resolution (The Conquest)

**As the** System
**I want to** calculate the total Power Rating committed to each territory every Sunday at 23:59 UTC, and award control to the highest contributing guild
**So that** there is a definitive, competitive endpoint to each week's effort.

## ✅ Acceptance Criteria (Gherkin)

### Scenario 1: Setting a Target Territory

- **Given** I am a Guild Leader
- **When** I click "Target" on the 'Iron Peaks' territory
- **And** we do not already have an Active Target set for this week
- **Then** 'Iron Peaks' is set as our Active Target
- **And** all guild members receive a notification about the new target.

### Scenario 2: Contributing Power to a Target

- **Given** my guild has 'Iron Peaks' as the Active Target
- **When** I complete a workout and earn 50 Power Rating
- **Then** 50 Power Rating is added to our guild's total sum for the 'Iron Peaks' conquest pool this week.

### Scenario 3: Weekly Resolution - Victory

- **Given** it is Sunday 23:59 UTC
- **And** Guild A has 5000 PR committed to 'Iron Peaks'
- **And** Guild B has 4500 PR committed to 'Iron Peaks'
- **When** the resolution cron job runs
- **Then** Guild A is awarded control of 'Iron Peaks'
- **And** Guild B loses control (if they held it previously)
- **And** all committed PR for 'Iron Peaks' is reset to 0 for the next week.

### Scenario 4: Max Territory Cap (Edge Case)

- **Given** Guild A already controls 3 Territories (the maximum limit)
- **When** they win the resolution for a 4th Territory ('Shadow Valley')
- **Then** they are awarded 'Shadow Valley'
- **But** they must automatically forfeit their oldest held territory so they remain at the cap of 3.

## 🛠️ Technical Notes & Architecture Implications

### Dependencies & Data Models

- A new `Territory` model is required (id, name, buffType, currentOwnerGuildId).
- A new `TerritoryConflict` or `GuildCommitment` model is required to track the weekly PR investments per guild per territory.
- **Dependency:** The existing Cron infrastructure (`/api/cron/heartbeat/route.ts`) will need a new module to handle the Sunday 23:59 resolution logic.

### Identified Risks

- **Snowball Effect:** The strongest guild might conquer all territories, freezing out smaller guilds.
  - *Mitigation:* The "Max 3 Territories" cap (Scenario 4) must be strictly enforced.
- **Performance:** Running a sum aggregation on thousands of users exactly at 23:59 could spike DB load.
  - *Mitigation:* Tally the score asynchronously during the week (e.g., increment the `GuildCommitment` row inside the `streak.ts` workout completion logic rather than summing raw logs on Sunday).
